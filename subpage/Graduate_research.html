01

A streamlined ChIP-exo 6.1 assay measures same-molecule positional relationship between transcription factors and nucleosomes in vivo
Abstract

Transcription factors (ssTFs) bind specific DNA sequences in promoter and enhancer regions in the context of an organized nucleosome architecture. There they recruit cofactors that regulated chromatin accessibility and transcription complex assembly. ChIP-exo is a high resolution genome-wide assay that measures protein-DNA interactions with single-bp precision. Here we couple the precision of ChIP-exo with a nuclease chromatin fragmentation strategy that cleaves and marks the rotationally exposed DNA on nucleosome surfaces. This new technique maps the positional orientation of TFs with an adjacent nucleosome surface on the same DNA molecule in human cells. Additional modifications to the ChIP-exo assay (version 6.1) greatly simplifies library construction over prior versions and provides a facile, high-precision means to map genomic TF binding sites.

 
 

02

Molecular architecture on cis-regulatory region in human genome
Abstract

Human gene transcription under the control from core promoter and cis-regulatory elements proximal or distal from their target genes. At those gene regulatory elements, there are motifs can be recognized by sequence specific transcription factor, where they can interact with other TF or cofactors to increase chromatin accessibility or help assembly of the transcription machinery, therefore augment the transcription level at transcription initiation step. Based on the hypothesis that tissue specific expression programs is related to tissue specific molecular composition and architecture on cis-regulatory elements, we are curious about the details of in-vivo interplay between sequence-specific transcription factors (TFs) and other proteins that involved in transcription initiation step, we would like to use ChIP-exo assays to reveal their positional organization within the complexes, therefore understanding how genomic and epigenomic information directs gene initiation at cell specific manner.

 
 

03

Single cell ChIP-exo by split-pool barcoding
Abstract

Most methods that study the landscape of TF binding including ChIP-exo assay measure the ensemble TF signals across millions of cells. Though protein-DNA interactions with single-bp precision can be achieved currently, it is still obscure critical information about the epigenome information of any given cell. In this research, we combine nuclei barcoding with ChIP-exo assay to profile TF binding at base-bp resolution as well as single cell resolution. Here in insoluble nuclei, the fragmented chromatins are lambda exonuclease digested and each cell gets a unique barcode by nuclei split-pool. After pooling barcoded nuclei for immunoprecipitation, the sequence information will demultiplex the TF profiling at single-cell resolution.
